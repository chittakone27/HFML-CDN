1. Good conditions in in-patient rooms  
    • Patient rooms regularly cleaned (cleaning schedule with dates, times and signatures on door)
    • Good ventilation without bad smells
    • Patients in beds cannot be seen from outside
2. Furniture available and in good state  
    • Beds have clean mattress and bed linen
    • Beds have mosquito nets
    • Each bed has a cupboard or small table for patient use
3. Inform the fee to clearly for patients can  
    • Name of the services in Lao and local language
    • Inform for Maternal and child cares are free
4. Patient room has light available during evening and night  
    • solar light or battery light back-up in case of power cut
5. Nursing is done by skilled staff  
    • check names and qualifications of nursing staff present during the assessment – at least 1 qualified nurse present in HF when patient is admitted
6. In-patient register available  
    • Names, addresses, date of birth, date of admission, date of discharge noted for each patient
7. Medical records of admitted patients present in the facility  
    • Name, date of birth, diagnosis, lab results, daily temperature, blood pressure, treatment info noted
5. CLINICAL CARE – CHILD HEALTH Answer
1. Number of infants <1 and children <5 in catchment area known
  Note down the date this number was last established and how  
    Date
    Method
2. Fridge available  
    • Presence of a fridge - temperature form available - filled twice a day
    • Temperature has remained between 2 and 8 degree Celsius
    • Verify functionality of thermometer and that temperature is indeed between 2 and 8 degree Celsius
    • No expired vaccines or drugs in the fridge
    • Readable labels on vials
3. Vaccine cold box or carrier available  
    • Cold box intact
    • Can be closed tightly
    • Foam pad for keeping cool during transport
    • Ice-packs available for transport
4. Immunisation according to schedule and properly recorded  
    • Randomly sample 5 immunisation records and check that all vaccinations have been given according to schedule
    • All vaccinations have been properly recorded (brand and dose of vaccine, date…)
5. Mothers of children <5 detected with malnutrition counselled  
    Sample 5 children <5 detected with malnutrition and verify that  
    • Correct notes have been made in the patient file on the content of counselling
6. Severely malnourished children followed-up  
    • Verify that follow-up information from the hospital is noted in the patient record/file, by using Z-score or MUAC or check the Ping book
7. Growth monitoring register correctly filled
8. All sick children <5 are checked for the 5 IMCI danger signs  
    Sample 5 children <5 from the OPD register and verify  
    • that they were checked for the following danger signs:  
    1. Lethargy (unconsciousness)
    2. Convulsion (now)
    3. History of convulsion in the past
    4. Vomiting everything
    5. Unable to drink or breastfeed
    • that any danger signs were managed immediately or that child was referred to higher level
    • that findings and treatment are recorded on the IMCI form
9. Children <5 with mild diarrhoea correctly treated  
    Sample 3 children <5 with diagnosis mild diarrhoea and verify that  
    • body weight was measured
    • Frequency of diarrhoea and vomiting, signs of dehydration were asked
    • treatment with Oralit solution was started
    • antibiotics (cotrimoxazole) given If signs of infection present
    • advice given to continue breast feeding (for infants only) all findings and treatment were noted in patient file
10. Children <5 with severe diarrhoea correctly treated  
    Sample 3 children <5 with diagnosis severe diarrhoea and verify that  
    • diagnosis was based on one or more of the following symptoms: diarrhoea > 3x/day; vomiting; signs of dehydration; bad skin turgor; sunken eyes; lethargy)
    • that findings and treatment are recorded in the child’s medical record/file
11. Children <5 with ARI correctly treated  
    Sample 5 children <5 with diagnosis ARI and verify that  
    • Diagnosis was based on following clinical findings: cough, fever, running nose, quick respiration
    • Child had NO dyspnoea, NO in-drawn chest
    • Treatment with antipyretics (paracetamol) and cough syrup WITHOUT antibiotics
    • Findings and treatment are recorded in child’s medical record
12. Children <5 with pneumonia correctly treated  
    Sample 3 children <5 with diagnosis pneumonia and verify that  
    • diagnosis was based on following clinical findings: cough and/or fever; quick respiration rate; dyspnoea; in-drawn chest
    • following treatment was given:  
            ✓ amoxicillin or cotrimoxazole by injection
            ✓ if no improvement occurred within 1-2 days child was referred to higher level
    • findings and treatment are recorded in the child’s patient file
6. CLINICAL CARE – MATERNAL HEALTH Answer
1. Number of women 15-49 in catchment area known
Note down the date this number was last established and how  
    • Date
    • Method
2. Necessary equipment for ANC available and functional  
    • Doppler sound detector or fetoscope, stethoscope, tape measure, scale, sphygmomanometer, haemoglobin meter available in the ANC room
    • Check functionality as far as possible
3. Availability of basic diagnostic kits for ANC  
    • urine test (for albumin), blood glucose rapid test
    • check that test kits are not expired
    • minimum stock to last 1 month - calculate on basis of number of pregnant women per year, divided by 12
4. Availability of additional diagnostic kits for ANC  
    • syphilis test, HIV rapid test and haemoglobin test present
    • check that test kits are not expired
    • minimum stock to last 1 month - calculate on basis of number of pregnant women per year, divided by 12
5. ANC administration in order  
    • Clinical Standards of Midwifery Practice available in ANC room 
    • At least 10 empty “pink books” present
6. First ANC visit done according to the protocol   
Sample 5 first ANC visits from the quarter and verify that:   
    • Parity and date of last menstruation noted in the pink book
    • Height, weight, HB, blood pressure, albumen and glucose in urine, height of fundus measured
    • Obstetrical examination of foetal heart beat done and presentation of foetus checked
    • Ferrous sulphate, folic acid given
    • tetanus toxoid vaccine given, if not received during previous pregnancy
    • All results are noted in the pink book
7. Fourth or last ANC visit done according to the protocol  
Sample 3 fourth (or last, if more than 4) ANC visits from the quarter and verify that the pregnant woman was checked for:  
    • Visit is during last month of pregnancy
    • Weight gain during pregnancy
    • height of the fundus, position of the foetus 
    • symptoms such as: bleeding, uterus contraction 
    • early Rupture of the Membrane  flow of amniotic fluid (ERM) 
    • albumin and glucose in the urine, blood pressure, HB
    • age of the pregnancy and expected date of delivery
    • all results are noted in the pink book 
8. Second PNC visit done according to the protocol  
Sample 3 second PNC visits from the quarter and verify that  
    • Check-up is done within 1 week after delivery
    • Mother checked for bleeding, fever, discharge, headache
    • Mother checked for breast engorgement and nipple rash
    • Baby checked for convulsions, fever, cyanotic symptoms
    • Baby checked for low weight, suckling reflex, icterus
    • Baby checked for rapid breathing (>60x/minute), drawn-in chest
    • All the results noted in the pink book
9. Information on different modern contraceptive methods available  
    • brochures, charts, or posters etc. available in ANC room and maternity 
    • with information on condom, IUD, OC, injectable, sterilisation (male and female) 
    • information on male and female reproductive system available  
10. Family planning methods available   
    • Stock of oral and injectable contraceptives and implants adequate for 1 month (based on historical use)  
    • Stock of condoms available  
11. FP administration in order  
    • FP register available and correctly and completely filled 
    • FP cards available enough for 1 month (based on historical use) 
12. Delivery room is well maintained   
    • Wall made from durable materials and painted  
    • Hard floor (preferably tiles), not damaged 
    • Windows have curtains, privacy is ascertained 
    • Clean (cleaning schedule with dates, times and signatures on the door)  
13. Delivery bed with removable cover and two functional leg supporters 
14. Delivery room has light during evening and night 
15. Running water is available or at least 20 litres for each delivery  
16. Waste is correctly collected and disposed off   
    • Waste bin for medical waste with lid present 
    • security box for needles present 
17. Delivery register and at least 10 partographs available 
18. For DH: Aspirator, fetoscope or Doppler, vacuum extractor available and functional   
        For HC: fetoscope or Doppler available and functional  
19. Sterile delivery kits and gloves available   
    • At least 2 delivery kits in DH and 1 in HC 
    • At least 25 pair of gloves in DH and 10 in HC 
    • Sterile string to tie umbilical cord  
20. All deliveries are done by skilled birth attendants   
    • Sample 3 deliveries from the delivery register over the past quarter and check names and qualifications of staff that attended
21. For the 3 sampled deliveries check that  
    • Partographs have been filled correctly, including Apgar scores of the baby at 1, 5 and 10 minutes after birth
    • Baby has been given Tetracycline eye-ointment
    • Baby has been given Hep B and BCG vaccines within 24 hours  
7. CLINICAL CARE – Infectious diseases Answer
1. Infectious disease protocols are present and easily accessible   
On Malaria, Pneumonia, TB, Typhoid Fever (GI), Dengue Fever,  
2. Malaria diagnosed and treated according to the protocol   
Sample 3 cases with malaria and verify that    
    • Diagnosis made using microscope or lab test (not on basis of fever)  
    • Treatment with ACT (Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy) 
3. Tuberculosis (TB) diagnosed and treated according to the protocol   
Sample 1 case with TB and verify that:   
    • Symptoms are correctly recorded in the patient form, such as: Lab test: Sputum positive for Mycobacteria, X-ray positive and Tuberculin test + (> 10 mm) 
    • Patients are correctly treated on time 
4. Gastro-intestinal (GI) infections diagnosed and treated according to the protocol   
Sample 1 case with GI infections (Typhoid fever – Salmonellosis) and verify that:   
    • Symptoms are recorded: high fever, abdominal ache, melena (black stool) 
    • Patients are correctly treated: Chloramphenicol 
5. Intestinal Worm infection: Ascaris and Hook worm (Ankylostomiasis) diagnosed and treated according to the protocol   
    • Symptoms correctly recorded in patient files   
            ✓ For ascaris: subfebrile, stomach ache, vomiting, children difficult to sleep 
            ✓ For Ankylostomiasis: anaemia, pitting edema, melena (GI bleeding) 
    • Patients are correctly treated with    
            ✓ albendazole (for ascaris) 
            ✓ pyrantel (for Ankylostomiasis) 
6. Dengue fever diagnosed and treated according to the protocol   
Sample 1 case with dengue and verify that:   
    • Diagnosis is made on basis of the following symptoms: fever, rash, characteristic aches, vomiting AND positive lab test 
    • Patient put on paracetemol (NOT asperin) and ORS 
8. CLINICAL CARE – Non-Communicable Diseases Answer
1. Hypertension and diabetes protocols present 
2. Hypertension diagnosis correctly made   
Sample 3 new hypertension cases and verify in the patient file that:   
    • 3 BP measurements were taken 
    • diagnosis of high blood pressure was correct : BP 140/90 or more at 3 measurements (when patient diabetic 135/85 or more)  
3. Hypertension managed according to protocol   
For the same 3 cases verify that   
    • they were counselled on lifestyle (stop smoking, lose weight, reduce salt intake, move more, limit alcohol intake) and observed for 1 month 
    • if after 1 month BP is still 140/90 or more treatment was started 
    • all findings noted in patient medical record/file
